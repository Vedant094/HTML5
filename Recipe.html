Humus Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 1/2 cup tahini (sesame seed paste)
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (about 1 large lemon)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Water or reserved chickpea cooking liquid, as needed
  • Paprika and chopped fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. If using dried chickpeas, soak them in water overnight. Drain and rinse before cooking. If using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse them.
  2. In a large pot, cover the soaked chickpeas with water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours until tender. If using canned chickpeas, skip this step.
  3. Drain the cooked chickpeas, reserving some of the cooking liquid if desired.
  4. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, cumin, and salt. Process until smooth and creamy. If the mixture is too thick, add water or reserved cooking liquid a tablespoon at a time until the desired consistency is reached.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with paprika and chopped parsley if desired.
  7. Serve with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or use as a spread in sandwiches.

Enjoy your homemade hummus!
